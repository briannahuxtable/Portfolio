Braccialetto

Mobile System & Wearable Device

Braccialetto is a wearable wayfinding device that provides communication assistance and is part of one’s embodiment. Braccialetto's goal is to help cyclist navigate to their destination with the vibrotactile signals being produced around the user’s body. This allows the users to navigate without any kind of visual distraction and improving awareness of the overall direction.

The concept the team proposed consists of three Lilypad vibe boards sewn on each wrist band, with an Arduino board and Parallax GPS sensor attached to a bike so that they can provide vibrotactile signals for cyclists when they have to change directions.

Cycling is a means of transportation that many of us utilize when travelling short and moderate distances. Visual attention to the surroundings is vital not only for safety but also for enhancing the cycling experience. Visual cues from navigation systems (GPS) may challenge the cyclists to directly experience and engage with the environment when they need to comprehend the surroundings to prevent them from getting lost. We developed Braccialetto to overcome these issues, and help with way finding.
